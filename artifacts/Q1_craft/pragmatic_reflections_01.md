## Software Engineer vs Data Engineer

The pragmatic philosophy transfers almost wholly, but its *axis* shifts.  
For software engineers, craftsmanship may end where code compiles and features behave.  
For data engineers and architects, craftsmanship also includes schema design, validation, lineage, and communication.

| Pragmatic Principle            | As a Software Engineer         | As a Data Engineer                                    |
| :----------------------------- | :----------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------- |
| Care about your craft          | Write clear, maintainable code | Guard data lineage and fidelity                       |
| Don’t live with broken windows | Fix bad code early             | Fix data rot early; document anomalies                |
| Provide options, not excuses   | Offer design alternatives      | Offer data trade-offs (accuracy vs latency)           |
| Make quality a requirement     | Test logic                     | Validate datasets and reproducibility                 |
| Communicate                    | Through code & docs            | Through code, docs, visual storytelling & translation |

**Essence:**  
> Pragmatic software aims for *working and functional code*.  
> Pragmatic data engineering aims for *trustworthy truth flow*.

## My Pragmatic Contract  
*A commitment to the engineer — and the person — I am becoming.*

### Principles I already embody
These are strengths that feel native to me, the principles I practice almost unconsciously:
1. **Care About My Craft**: I show up with seriousness, curiosity, and intention.
2. **Think About My Work**: I engage with problems conceptually, not mechanically.
3. **You Have Agency**: I act to improve my surrounding.
4. **Remember the Big Picture**: I perceive systems, not fragments.
5. **Invest Regularly in My Knowledge Portfolio**: learning is part of the rhythm of my life.

### Principles I am committed to deepen
These traits resonate with my values but deserve more deliberate cultivation:
1. **Provide Options, Don’t Make Lame Excuses**: replace any justification with clarity and paths forward.
2. **Don’t Live With Broken Windows**: prevent the slow decay of systems, teams, and discipline.
3. **Be a Catalyst for Change**: influence by example, not pressure.
4. **Make Quality a Requirements Issue**: treat data trust and reproducibility as part of the spec.
5. **Critically Analyze What You Read and Hear**: apply second-order thinking consistently.
6. **Build Documentation In, Don’t Bolt It On**: integrate narrative and reasoning into every artifact.

## What “Pragmatic” Means to Me  
*A personal definition, beyond code.*

Pragmatism is not just a stance toward software.  
It’s a **relationship to reality**, the living systems and human layers the code ultimately serves.

For me, to be pragmatic means:
- designing with awareness of the *team*, not just my own flow;  
- building systems that respect stakeholders’ time and trust;  
- choosing solutions that serve the company’s direction, not only my technical preferences;  
- shaping my career intentionally, without drifting;  
- organizing my work so that it elevates my life rather than consuming it.

In other words:

> **Pragmatic means creating outcomes that are aligned with truth, with people, and with the long arc of my own becoming.**

It is intelligence in service of meaning.

## Temperament & Craftsmanship

Rough analysis of my craftsmanship style based on results from my Fisher's personality/temperament profile.

**Director’s clarity.** 
You perceive systems structurally; coherence is your native language. 
This yields precision and foresight, though it can delay playful experimentation. 
Keep a sandbox where disorder is allowed.

**Explorer’s curiosity.** 
Learning is your oxygen. It fuels innovation but can diffuse focus.

**Negotiator’s warmth.** 
You bring relational harmony into technical environments. 
This makes collaboration fluid; clarity becomes invitation, not imposition.

**Aesthetic awareness.**  
Elegance matters. You feel when a pipeline “flows right.” 
That sensitivity transforms engineering into quiet art.
